Memory is how Juicebox personalizes AI-generated work (searches, criteria, sequences, and Agents) to the way you and your team actually recruit. You can review and edit everything Juicebox has learned from the **Memory** tab in your account settings.

To open it, click your name/organization at the bottom of the left-hand menu, select **Account Settings**, then open the **Memory** tab.

![The Memory tab in Account Settings](/images/memory/memory-tab-location.png)

## Types of memory

Juicebox stores two kinds of memory:

- **Profile and Org Context**: background research about you and your organization, gathered automatically when you sign up.
- **Usage Memory**: preferences and patterns Juicebox picks up from the searches and inputs you run day-to-day.

Both feed into your searches, sequences, and Agents so that results reflect what your team is actually looking for, without you having to repeat yourself in every prompt.

## Profile and Org Context

When you sign up, Juicebox automatically runs deep research online about:

- **You**: your role, background, and other publicly available context.
- **Your organization**: what your company does, and other publicly available context.

This is general background information about you and your org, not recruiting-specific preferences. Juicebox uses it as context for **Search**, **Sequences**, and **Agents**.

### Notifications when memory updates

Whenever Juicebox adds to or updates your Profile and Org Context, you'll get an in-app notification. Open the notification to review what changed.

### Editing memory

You can edit Profile and Org Context at any time from the **Memory** tab:

- Update or remove entries that are out of date or inaccurate.
- Add context Juicebox may have missed (for example, a new business line, a hiring focus, or a preferred candidate profile).

Changes take effect immediately and apply to future searches, sequences, and Agent runs.

## Usage Memory

Usage Memory captures preferences Juicebox learns from how you actually use the product, including the searches you run, the criteria you add, the profiles you shortlist or hide, and the feedback you give.

Over time, this lets Juicebox anticipate the requirements you tend to care about without you having to spell them out every time.

Usage Memory is maintained automatically in the background. It isn't directly editable from the **Memory** tab, only Profile and Org Context can be reviewed and edited there.

### Criteria

Usage Memory surfaces directly in [criteria](/criteria). When Juicebox adds a criterion based on what it has learned from your past searches, it's labeled **criteria added by memory**.

Hover the memory icon next to a criterion to see the specific memory that influenced it.
