### Introduction

Email sequences have powerful tracking built in. Use your sequence activity to identify successful campaigns and promising strategies. This guide will cover the detailed sequence view, as well as our analytics features built in.

![Screenshot 2025-06-23 at 3.58.57 PM](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/juiceboxwork/docs/kb-migration/images/sequence-tracking/Screenshot_2025-06-23_at_3-58-57_PM.png)

### Sequence Details

To view the details on an email sequence, simply click the sequence on the Email Sequences tab. You will then open the sequence tracking view.

![Screenshot 2025-06-23 at 5.02.11 PM](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/juiceboxwork/docs/kb-migration/images/sequence-tracking/Screenshot_2025-06-23_at_5-02-11_PM.png)

![Group 33608](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/juiceboxwork/docs/kb-migration/images/sequence-tracking/Group_33608.png)

The view includes:

- A list of all profiles added into the sequence
- Their respective statuses (active, cancelled, unsubscribed, etc.)
- Attribution data
- Engagement data (open rate, click rate)

You can click into any name to open the detailed profile view, showing you the specific email that was sent to the candidate.

![Sequence Tracking](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/juiceboxwork/docs/kb-migration/images/sequence-tracking/Untitled.png)

### Labeling Profile Responses

You can label email replies directly within the Sequences dashboard as _Interested_ or _Not Interested_. When a recipient replies to your email, select _Mark as Replied_, then choose their response status. These labels sync automatically to the Shortlist page, allowing you to filter and review profiles based on interest.

![Screenshot 2025-07-01 at 4.46.48 PM](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/juiceboxwork/docs/kb-migration/images/sequence-tracking/Screenshot_2025-07-01_at_4-46-48_PM.png)

### Sequence Analytics

With each sequence, you receive six different data points:

- Total number of profiles in sequence
- Active
- Finished
- Replied
- Opened
- Bounced

When hovering over the figures, corresponding percentages are shown.
